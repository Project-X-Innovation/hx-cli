## Description
I want to add the tiniest bit of governance to the deploy flow. Often we have four or five developers putting out four or five Helix tickets at a time. I want to give the option to have another teammate approve a ticket before it is merged to staging.
Right because once it's in staging, then it goes into production at the next deploy. It's before merging to staging that is really the last chance.

I don't want to get too deep into this feature of forcing people to get approval before staging. I don't want to do that at this junction. Maybe in the future you can remind them to get approval. You can force them to get approval.

At this point I would just have it there on the ticket as approved by. I would also give the option to ask for approval or we can just use normal messaging. I think for an MVP we can just use normal messaging so there should be an approver, approved by. It's a prover, though much, just approved by. It shouldn't actually stop anyone from deploying or merging if they don't approve but it's just an optional thing to have and it should be there on the ticket approved by. It's not to slow people down but it's just to have the direction be coherent. The idea is that the direction of the product should be coherent. If everybody's adding all their features and taking it in a different direction, then it just becomes a spaghetti monster. I want everyone to contribute and I want the velocity to keep growing but it just adds a little bit of extra proof if somebody else approved it.
